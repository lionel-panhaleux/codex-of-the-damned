{% trans trimmed %} These are guides to help you decide what to buy. Your choices will depend primarily on whether you're a Neonate joining the night or an Elder coming out of torpor. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Neonates joining the night') }}

{% trans trimmed v5_box=external("https://www.blackchantry.com/vampire-the-eternal-struggle-fifth-edition/", _("V5 box")) %} Neonates are the new Methuselah joining us in the Eternal Struggle. If you're looking into getting in the game, your best bet is without question the {{ v5_box }}, containing 5 Camarilla preconstructed decks: Ventrue {{ ventrue }}, Tremere {{ tremere }}, Toreador {{ toreador }}, Nosferatu {{ nosferatu }} and Malkavian {{ malkavian }}. You can play them straight out of the box and get a few fun games out of them. It is the perfect start for a collection or a curious Coterie alike. {% endtrans %}

{% trans trimmed %} If you don't have access to the box or it is out of print in your area, don't panic! {% trans trimmed %} From there, two possibilities are open to you: Go wide or Go deep. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Go wide: more clans, more decks') }}

{% trans trimmed %} You might want to acquire more decks featuring more clans. This would give you and, maybe, your playgroup, more options and allow for a lot of different games. {% endtrans %}

{% trans trimmed %} It really depends on the kind of players you are: some playgroups prefer to have each member focus on their own clan and try to improve their deck game after game, while others prefer to mix and match, switching clan and deck every game. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('The V5 format') }}

{% trans trimmed v5_format=external("https://www.blackchantry.com/2025/09/16/introducing-the-official-vampire-the-eternal-struggle-v5-format/", _("official V5 format")) %} The {{ v5_format }} is now a real thing. Made official in September 2025, it restricts deckbuilding to the modern V5 products — the Fifth Edition preconstructed decks, the New Blood packs, Fall of London, Shadows of Berlin, the 30th Anniversary set and a specific handful of promo cards — while leaving out the legacy products and the print-on-demand reprints. The linked announcement spells out exactly which products and promos are in, and which are out. {% endtrans %}

{% trans trimmed %} It is an alternate format, not a replacement: it exists to give new playgroups and game shops a self-contained, easy-to-buy cardpool. The Standard Constructed format — the one using every card ever printed — remains the format of the biggest events (nationals, continentals and Grand Prix), and it is still by far the more popular of the two. {% endtrans %}

{% trans trimmed %} Contrarily to other CCGs, the more time goes by, the more reprints get available for all the old cards through the print-on-demand system, so new joiners face less and less difficulty acquiring the cards they need for the standard format. {% endtrans %}
