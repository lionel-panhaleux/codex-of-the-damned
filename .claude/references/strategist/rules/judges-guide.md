# VTES Tournament Conduct & Infraction Guide

## 1. General Philosophy

Judges: impartial overseers. Intervene when asked, when breach observed, or to calm situations. Respond to issues after occurrence. May act proactively on registration, deck checks, card legality.

Penalties are corrective/educational, not punitive. If players resolve minor errors among themselves fairly, no judge needed. Significant problems require identifying infraction category and following procedures.

Only Head Judge may deviate from this guide, only in extraordinary circumstances. Routine factors (round, player skill/age, leniency) never justify deviation. Any judge considering exception must consult Head Judge first.

Judge errors: acknowledge and correct if possible. If player violated rules due to flawed judge info, Head Judge may reduce penalty. Head Judge may authorize roll-back to moment of mistaken advice if practical.

English version is definitive for interpretation disputes.

### 1.1. Penalties

#### 1.1.1. Caution
Lightest penalty. For very minor, immediately fixable infractions with no lasting disruption or strategic advantage. Verbal only. Must include: what was violated, correct procedure, consequences if repeated. No formal tracking required, but inform Head Judge.

#### 1.1.2. Warning
Formally tracked. For incorrect play/procedural problems requiring time to fix, or minor issues that escalated. Purposes: notify of problem, ensure permanent record in V:EKN Penalty Database, discourage repeats. Must be: communicated clearly, explained in detail, reported to Head Judge, submitted for tracking. Time extension if resolution >1 minute (see 1.2.4).

#### 1.1.3. Standings Adjustment
Severe, formally tracked. Beyond Warning but not DQ-worthy. Imposes -1 VP tournament-level scoring consequence.

**When applied:**
- During ongoing game: -1 VP to that game
- Between preliminary rounds (or between last prelim and final): -1 VP to next game
- After last preliminary round: -1 VP to previous game
- Before round 1 pairings: -1 VP to round 1

Does not directly change GW/TP, but -1 VP may alter who gets GW, which changes TP.

**Example 1:** Player wins 3-2, gets -1 VP -> 2 VP. If tied for highest, becomes 2-2 tie (no GW). TP per tie rules.

**Example 2:** Player A sweeps 5-player table (5VP+GW). Player E (0VP) gets SA -> -1 VP. Player A gets 60 TP. Three players tied 2nd-4th get (48+36+24)/3=36 TP each. Player E in 5th gets 12 TP.

Must be communicated, explained, reported to Head Judge, submitted to V:EKN Penalty Database. Time extension if investigation >1 minute.

#### 1.1.4. Disqualification (DQ)
Most serious. Always includes Warning. Player removed from event. Issued for: deliberate cheating, threatening competitive integrity, serious/repeated unsportsmanlike conduct, obstructive/abusive behavior, endangering others.

**Key details:**
- Player loses current round, dropped from tournament
- Prizes reassigned per final standings; unawarded prizes pass to next eligible; already-distributed participation items not reclaimed
- Applies to non-players (spectators, staff) too
- Proof not required; Head Judge determines enough info exists to conclude integrity at risk
- Must be submitted to penalty database
- DQs from intentional acts compromising integrity/safety: report to VEKN Ethics Committee. DQs from accumulated unintentional infractions: no escalation required

**DQ effects:** Removed from standings entirely. Remaining players move up.

**Game state after DQ:**

1. **Remove immediately:** Turn ends. All minions, cards, pool, hand, uncontrolled removed. Ongoing effects end unless card explicitly states otherwise.
2. **No VP awarded:** Predator gets no VP. No pool gained. DQ is administrative removal, not oust.
3. **Table position:** Predator and prey become adjacent. Effects depending on seating order adapt.
4. **Restarting table:** Strongly discouraged. Only for 5-player games when DQ very early (before/during first turns) and removing player destroys competitive integrity.
   - Preliminary: Reseat as 4-player, rebuild/reshuffle decks
   - Final: Must remain 5 players. Next highest-ranked player on premises fills seat. Full pre-final seating process redone.
5. **Edge cases:** Benefits from earlier legal actions involving DQ'd player remain unless illegal. Avoid cascading corrections unless clear unfair advantage. If DQ from corrupted game state (cheating), Head Judge may roll back per Section 1.4.
6. **Judge discretion:** Head Judge may deviate if standard fix produces inequitable result or timing interacts disastrously with multiplayer dynamics. Must explain to table and report in DQ report.

### 1.2. Applying Penalties

Applies to all sanctioned events. Apply penalties ASAP, ideally same round. After tournament concluded and approved, no retroactive penalties except proven intentional misconduct via VEKN Ethics Committee.

#### 1.2.1. Escalation

Escalation sequence: **Caution -> Caution -> Warning -> Warning -> Standings Adjustment -> Standings Adjustment -> Disqualification**

Baseline Caution: 1st=Caution, 2nd=Caution, 3rd=Warning, 4th=Warning, 5th=SA...
Baseline Warning: 1st=Warning, 2nd=Warning, 3rd=SA, 4th=SA, 5th=DQ
Baseline SA: 1st=SA, 2nd=SA, 3rd=DQ

DQ from accumulation: not "DQ without Prize."

Escalation resets per event. Prior Warnings for same infraction visible to judges as context (treated collectively as single pre-existing Warning, doesn't advance current escalation). Persistent patterns across events may warrant closer scrutiny or reclassification under Unsportsmanlike Conduct.

DQ from accumulation must be based on infractions within same event.

#### 1.2.2. Issuing Significant Penalties
SA or DQ: communicate to Head Judge. Recommended only Head Judge issue these (except clear decklist errors). Non-players/unregistered can receive penalties; must be temporarily added to roster with VEKN number. Penalties remain valid even if player drops/withdraws.

#### 1.2.3. Communication
Judge must explain: (1) what the infraction was, (2) how game state corrected, (3) what penalty is including escalation. Head Judge deviations must explain standard remedy and reason for deviation.

#### 1.2.4. Time Extensions
Compensate time lost to judge intervention, not normal game discussion.

Method (extensions, clock stops, or both) announced by Head Judge at tournament start.

**Clock stopping options:** Auto-stop when judge called; stop only when judge instructs; no stops (use extensions only).

**Grant extension when:** Judge spent >=1 min at table; delay from procedures players couldn't control (Slow Play/GRV investigation, player questions needing judge, dispute resolution, penalty explanation).

**Don't grant for:** Normal table talk, slow decisions (game pace), unnecessary questions, late arrival.

**Duration baseline:**
- 30-60s ruling: none
- 1-3 min: +2 min
- 3-5 min: +3-5 min
- >5 min: +5-10 min

Never add more than actually lost. Extensions stack. Cap at 10 min total unless major disruption or Head Judge authorizes more.

#### 1.2.5. Consistency
Follow written procedures exactly. Don't "improve" penalties based on game state, seating, strategic balance. Consistent procedures protect judges from bias accusations.

#### 1.2.6. Linked Infractions
Single error -> multiple infractions: issue only the most severe.

#### 1.2.7. Assessing Intent
VTES is political/asymmetrical; suboptimal/counterintuitive plays are often valid. Outcome alone never determines intent.

**Principles:** Assume good faith. Evaluate patterns, not isolated actions. Consider game context (pool, position, timing, archetype, remaining time). Give player opportunity to explain; explanation need only be plausible.

**Intent commonly relevant for:** Not Playing to Win, Stalling vs legitimate Slow Play, Collusion vs Political Cooperation, Cheating vs Procedural Error, Aggressive Behaviour vs Emotional Frustration, Rage-Quitting vs Forced Drop.

**Don't:** Punish poor play. Infer intent from table talk alone. Escalate on speculation.

**When unclear:** Ask neutral questions. Observe subsequent behavior. Prefer least severe ruling. Document for pattern tracking. Only apply intent-based penalties (SA/DQ) when intent reasonably established.

### 1.3. Randomizing a Library
Before re-randomizing: determine if any part is known/arranged. Consult players. Check ash heap, removed cards, cards in play that may have influenced order. Separate known-position cards, shuffle randomized portion, return fixed cards to proper locations. Judge shuffles for rulings don't count as game shuffles and can't trigger card effects.

### 1.4. Game State Corrections

#### 1.4.1. Rolling Back
Roll-back = reverse game to exact moment before erroneous action. Must be complete and sequential (strict reverse order, no steps skipped). Only Head Judge may approve (passes to secondary judge if Head Judge is player in that game, then third judge, etc.).

Requirements: no player's decisions meaningfully shaped by info gained after error point; reconstructed sequence would plausibly unfold same way. Only attempt with very few subsequent choices/hidden effects.

Execution: reverse each action in precise reverse order. Unknown cards returned to hidden area -> judge selects random card from legal candidates. Cards revealed due to error returned to library and shuffled (unless subsequently drawn into hand).

Roll-backs with hidden/random elements: extreme caution. Avoid returning cards to library when player controls search/shuffle effects unless absolutely necessary.

**Simple roll-back:** Reverses only most recent game action. No randomization. Used only when small correction cleanly resolves situation.

If roll-back requires reconstructing multiple branching decisions, heavy randomization, unwinding several turns, or altering decisions based on now-public info -> use Partial Game State Correction (1.4.2).

#### 1.4.2. Partial Game State Corrections
For illegal states discovered long after original error. Applied when strict roll-back can't be safely executed AND leaving illegal state would grant continuing advantage.

Not a roll-back. Must not reverse action sequences or randomize past draws. Removes/neutralizes only currently illegal elements, altering minimum other elements.

**Principles:**
1. Remove illegal element (card to ash heap, correct improperly modified attributes if no unwinding needed)
2. Minimize collateral changes (only minimum necessary)
3. Don't recreate past game flow (accept "as played")
4. Avoid randomization unless illegal element in hidden area that can't legally remain
5. Head Judge approval required

## 2. Procedural Errors

Covers mistakes from incorrect game procedure execution, not cheating/unsportsmanlike conduct. Strict escalation due to exploitation potential.

### 2.1. Missed Mandatory Effect
**Def:** Player fails to demonstrate awareness of mandatory effect from their card before it should visibly impact game.

**Examples:** Forget to burn pool for Anarch Revolt; ready vampire with no blood must hunt first but player bleeds with another first; plays Charismatic Aura not realizing Dark Influences will cancel it.

If intentional, see 4.7 Cheating.

**Penalty:** Caution. Upgraded to **Warning** if effect is detrimental to controller or missing it creates advantage.

**Remedy:** If resolvable without major disruption, apply immediately. Otherwise continue as-is.

### 2.2. Card Access Error
**Def:** Player gains access to cards not entitled to see/draw, or fails to access required cards. Includes extra draws, too few draws, incorrect timing, initial draw errors.

**Examples:** Draw 2 instead of 1; reveal top card while shuffling; forget end-of-minion-phase draw; wrong initial hand size; fail to draw from resolved effect; draw before announcing effects; replace card when opponent wanted time to consider cancelling.

Intentional -> 4.7 Cheating.

**Penalty:** Caution.

**Remedy:** Reverse/mitigate info gain. Return identifiable extra cards to hidden area + shuffle. Unidentifiable extras -> remove random from hand, shuffle into library. Complete required draws. Head Judge decides unusual cases.

### 2.3. Game Rule Violation
**Def:** Player violates VTES rules or performs procedure incorrectly, not covered by another category.

**Examples:** Incorrect blood cost; plays Conditioning but declares Bonding's effect; performs disallowed action; plays card without meeting requirements (Art Museum without ready Toreador); Parity Shift with no legal target; card in wrong area.

**Penalty:** **Caution** if fixable with simple roll-back or correct declaration. **Warning** if not.

**Remedy hierarchy:**
1. Simple roll-back (no major decisions made, no hidden info changed)
2. Partial fix: failed unlock same turn, illegal mandatory choice, card in wrong area, illegally played card shuffled back
3. Structured forward fix: remove advantage (unlock improperly locked minions, restore costs, return resources, randomize hidden areas)

If declared card legal and in hand but wrong physical card placed: treat declared card as played if: declaration unambiguous, no replacement drawn, no hidden info revealed, no decisions depended on physical card.

Opponents who could have noticed may get 2.4 Failure to Maintain Game State.

### 2.4. Failure to Maintain Game State
**Def:** Player notices a visible, relevant, ongoing Game Rule Violation and reasonably could speak up but doesn't. Requires evidence player actually noticed. Doesn't apply to missed mandatory effects.

**Examples:** Sees predator bleed with locked vampire, says nothing; sees opponent play second master without extra MPA, stays silent; notices non-repeatable action done twice; sees unpaid Claws of the Dead cost; sees illegal transfer amount.

**Penalty:** **Standings Adjustment**. Never escalates.

## 3. Tournament Errors

### 3.1. Deck Problems

#### 3.1.1. Illegal Decklist
Only at tournaments requiring decklists. Illegal if: below format minimum, banned/illegal cards, incorrect crypt grouping, unclear/ambiguous entries.

**Examples:** 58 library cards (min 60); lists banned Madness of the Bard; writes "Haven" (ambiguous); lists Arika (G2) + Lutz (G4); loses cards mid-event.

**Penalty:** **Warning**. Upgraded to **Standings Adjustment** if both deck and decklist illegal and can't be reconciled without substantially altering deck (player gets 5 min to adjust).

If shorthand clearly/unambiguously identifies a card, penalty may be reduced.

Errors found outside a game apply at start of next round unless actual deck was illegal.

**Remedy:** Remove banned/illegal cards. Correct unambiguous entries. If short cards, add basic filler (Blood Doll, Wake with Evening's Freshness etc.) to reach minimum; may revert if originals found. Modify decklist to match legal deck if no misconduct concealed.

**Incorrect crypt grouping:** Head Judge corrects to legal config. Substituted vampire must be legal, unambiguous, no more advantageous. Neutral subs (Anarch Convert) only if no strategic benefit. If illegal vampire already in play: replace with legal vampire of equal/greater capacity; excess pool not refunded; blood adjusted to avoid advantage; if no fair correction, remove from play.

#### 3.1.2. Illegal Main Deck (Legal Decklist)
Decklists in use. Deck illegal but decklist legal: wrong card count, illegal cards, wrong crypt grouping, doesn't match decklist, misplaced cards from previous games.

**Examples:** 59 cards with 60 on list; Anthelios (banned) in deck; Enkidu (G4) + Beast (G2); opponent's card mixed in; offensive altered card.

If listed card has requirements incompatible with deck crypt but actual card matches (e.g., "Campground HG" listed but "Zoo HG" in deck for Gangrel-only crypt), correct decklist instead.

**Penalty:** **Standings Adjustment**. Player must adjust deck to match list (or vice versa) before next round.

**Remedy:** Retrieve out-of-place cards. Identify/reveal incorrect cards, replace with correct ones. More removals than additions -> remove non-library cards first; if still needed, replace with random library cards. Missing card from another player's deck -> penalties to both.

#### 3.1.3. Illegal Main Deck (No Decklists)
No decklists used. Illegal if: wrong card count, illegal cards, wrong crypt grouping, improper proxies/Dark Pack cards.

**Penalty:** **Standings Adjustment**. Player must make deck legal before next round.

**Remedy:** Remove illegal cards. If below minimum, add filler using one consistent method:
- Neutral filler (Burn Option, Anarch Convert) to minimum
- Copies of highest-frequency library card
- Cards in descending frequency order
Filler must be proxy cards from organizers, returned/discarded after event. Never add strategic counter-cards. Oversized -> remove random cards. Incorrect crypt grouping: same rules as 3.1.1.

### 3.2. Outside Assistance
**Def:** After game starts, providing/receiving strategic, tactical, or hidden-info input that could influence ongoing game decisions, or referencing pre-prepared strategic materials during round.

**Not OA:** Reviewing own decklist; taking in-game notes (if no slow play); discussing finals seating before sitting; natural table talk among seated players.

**Is OA:** Strategic/tactical advice from/to non-seated persons; sharing hidden info (hand, uncontrolled, top card, master phase options); referencing pre-round notes/documents/guides (except card text and own decklist). Also applies during draft. No notes during draft. Pre-round notes only referenced between games.

**Examples:** Asking friend for play advice; spectator reveals opponent's hand; secret note-taking during draft; texting teammate for play advice; eliminated player signaling threats to friend at same table.

Minor card annotations for game text OK; detailed strategy notes on cards not OK. Head Judge decides acceptable markings. Spectators may be removed from venue.

**Responsibility:** Provider of unsolicited assistance = primary offender. Player who solicits/accepts/benefits also commits infraction. Both may be penalized. Player who immediately rejects and calls judge may have penalty mitigated/waived.

**Penalty:** **Standings Adjustment** to offending party/parties.

### 3.3. Slow Play
**Def:** Taking significantly more time than reasonably necessary for game actions/decisions.

If deliberate time manipulation to influence outcome -> 4.6 Stalling.

**Examples:** Repeatedly rereading ash heap/uncontrolled without game state changes; excessive hesitation on basic decisions; unreasonable shuffle time; leaving table without judge approval; consistently delaying action targets.

**Penalty:** **Caution**. Judges should say "Please play faster" not "stalling." Time extension if significant round time consumed.

### 3.4. Limited Procedure Violation
**Def:** Technical/procedural mistake during draft (handling boosters, picks, timing incorrectly). Draft formats only.

**Examples:** Pass wrong direction; exceed pick time; try to change pick after placing; looking toward others' hands/piles.

**Penalty:** **Caution**. If error gained unintended info, may fall under Card Access Error or Outside Assistance.

**Remedy:** Correct draft flow. Restore booster/card if possible. Remind pod of procedures. Time extension only if judge intervention meaningfully slowed pod.

### 3.5. Public Information Miscommunication
**Def:** Providing incorrect/misleading/incomplete info about publicly available game elements.

**Examples:** Misstate pool count; misstate blood on vampire; claim nonexistent intercept bonus; announce wrong bleed amount due to ignored bonus; misstate Edge holder.

**Penalty:** **Warning**. Repeated violations never escalate unless significantly impacting gameplay.

**Remedy:** May roll-back to affected action (not to moment of miscommunication). Use carefully to avoid revealing info.

### 3.6. Obscuring Game State
**Def:** Creating/maintaining ambiguous physical layout that obscures public info. Covers unclear presentation, not incorrect verbal communication (that's 3.5).

Includes: overlapping/stacking cards hiding info; non-standard positions blurring controlled/uncontrolled/locked/ready/torpor; unclear minion-equipment associations; ambiguous counter placement; persistent disorganization after instruction.

**Penalty:** **Caution**.

**Remedy:** Reorganize play area immediately into clear layout.

### 3.7. Marked Cards
**Def:** Cards/sleeves show physical inconsistencies allowing identification in library/crypt/hand. Includes scuffs, bends, fading, marks, curvature, sleeve/thickness differences. Opaque sleeves: evaluate sleeves. Clear sleeves: evaluate both.

**Two severities:**
- **Minor (no pattern):** Marks distributed randomly, no correlation to card type/function
- **Patterned (significant):** Marks correlate to card categories or impactful cards

**Penalty:**
- Minor: **Warning**
- Patterned: **Standings Adjustment**

**Remedy:** Replace marked sleeves/cards ASAP. Head Judge may issue proxies for damaged cards with clear sleeves. If can't fix, re-sleeve entire deck. If can't bring deck to legal/unmarked/uniform state -> removed from event. If deck modified because cards can't be replaced -> SA + decklist updated.

### 3.8. Insufficient Shuffling
**Def:** Unintentionally fails to sufficiently shuffle library/crypt before presenting for cut. Deck not shuffled if judge believes player could know position/distribution of cards. Includes forgetting to shuffle after search effect or using inadequate procedures.

**Examples:** Forget shuffle after Magic of the Smith; single riffle after search; patterned pile-shuffle without follow-up randomization.

Patterned pile-shuffling only permitted at game start. Stacking/weaving acceptable only if followed by thorough randomization. Intentional manipulation -> 4.7 Cheating.

**Penalty:** **Warning**.

**Remedy:** Thoroughly shuffle affected portion (or full shuffle if judge deems necessary).

## 4. Unsportsmanlike Conduct

### 4.1. Minor
**Def:** Disruptive, disrespectful, emotionally charged behavior that is non-targeted, situational, easily correctable. Brief etiquette lapses (raised voices, rude remarks, outbursts) that negatively impact table but don't create hostile/unsafe atmosphere.

**Examples:** Overly hostile tone during rules discussion; explicit/provocative/inflammatory playmat/sleeves; improperly demanding penalty for another player; appealing to Head Judge without letting floor judge rule first; slamming table after setback; eye-rolling/sarcastic remarks; brief intimidating posture; leaving trash in play area; failing to follow official instruction to leave; repeatedly interrupting others' negotiations; offensive altered card content.

**Penalty:** **Warning**. Must correct behavior immediately.

### 4.2. Major
**Def:** Targeted, aggressive, coercive actions creating hostile/unsafe/intimidating environment. Persistent verbal aggression, personal insults (race, religion, gender, orientation, disability, etc.), intimidation, harassment. Impact on others is determining factor, not intent. Physical violence threats -> 4.3.

**Examples:** Racial/discriminatory slurs; threatening body language to pressure deals; demeaning comments about play skill/personal traits; sustained personal attacks; aggressive posture/tone making target feel unsafe; repeated misgendering; unauthorized photos/videos; unwanted personal interactions; intentionally blocking movement; social media harassment during event.

**Penalty:** **Standings Adjustment**. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

**Upgrade to DQ** if: malicious intent, no remorse, or reoffense. Must report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

### 4.3. Aggressive Behaviour
**Def:** Physically threatening, violent, or intentionally damaging behavior toward persons, belongings, or tournament property.

**Examples:** Threatening physical harm; pulling chair causing fall; threatening judge; destroying others' cards; flipping/striking table; spectator aggressively confronting player.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Remove from venue. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee if warranted.

**Table-flipping remedy:** Halt game, ensure safety. If game state reliably reconstructable (visible cards, known totals, unanimous agreement) -> resume with time extension. If not -> terminate game. Avoid penalizing remaining players. Document everything.

### 4.4. Bribery and Wagering
**Def:** Offering/requesting/hinting at/accepting out-of-game incentives to influence game/match outcome (concessions, altered play, oust order, VP manipulation). Includes trading prizes/cards/favors for results. Wagering = betting on any tournament outcome (doesn't need to be monetary, bettor needn't be in that game).

**Examples:** Cash to concede; rare card for unopposed oust; concession for prize share; winner gets card from loser; spectators wagering on VPs; intentional draw for non-game benefits.

In-game negotiation is fundamental to VTES; negotiation about match results/concessions/prize outcomes is prohibited.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

### 4.5. Theft of Tournament Material
**Def:** Taking/concealing/retaining materials from tournament environment or other participants.

**Examples:** Removing cards from another's deck between rounds; taking tournament property; hiding opponent's card found after round; pocketing communal counters.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Remove from venue. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee if warranted.

### 4.6. Stalling
**Def:** Intentionally delaying game progress to exploit round time limit. If unintentional -> 3.3 Slow Play.

Key indicator: **behavior change**, especially when in advantageous position or near end of game. Consistent pace throughout shouldn't be forced to change.

**Examples:** Prolonged "thinking" on trivial decisions while leading; deliberately slowing influence/minion phase while ahead in VP; prolonging decisions when about to be ousted to run clock; excessive time on mandatory effects without purpose; switching from shortcuts to full step-by-step in final minutes (e.g., "ready for strikes" to "nothing before range, waiting on response, no maneuver, waiting on response...").

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Remove from venue if instructed.

### 4.7. Cheating
**Def:** Intentionally violating rules/policies, lying to officials, or becoming aware of significant offense and not reporting it. Requires BOTH: (1) attempting to gain advantage AND (2) awareness action is illegal. If either missing, handle as another infraction.

**Examples:** Altering game report; lying to judge about game state; knowingly allowing opponent's error that benefits you; not calling attention to opponent's missed mandatory effect that benefits you; secretly viewing others' crypt/library; adding unregistered cards to deck; knowing about extra draw/manipulation and not calling judge.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Remove from venue. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

### 4.8. Fraud
**Def:** Intentionally misrepresenting rules, procedures, personal info, or tournament-relevant info.

**Examples:** False name/VEKN ID; falsely claiming card works differently; explaining procedures incorrectly for advantage; pretending to misunderstand game state to justify illegal action; hiding extra boosters at limited event; forging/altering Archon sheet; misreporting game result.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

### 4.9. Collusion
**Def:** Players deliberately agree to alter/predetermine/illegally establish game/match outcomes outside normal permitted negotiation.

**Examples:** Letting friend oust without resistance; coin flip to decide concession; cross-table coordinated concessions; pre-round agreement on who "takes the table."

**A deal is NOT Collusion if ALL true:**
- No out-of-game incentives (money, cards, favors)
- Everything discussed openly at table
- No random method (coin flip, die roll, card draw) to determine winner/concession
- No predetermined tournament outcomes beyond normal legal play

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Report to VEKN Ethics Committee.

### 4.10. Health & Safety Disruption
**Def:** Physical condition/behavior creating unsafe/unsanitary/severely uncomfortable environment (extreme hygiene, intoxication, health/safety risk).

**Penalty:** **Warning -> Removal from venue if uncorrected**. Private request to correct first. If severe/uncorrectable, remove from tournament with no additional penalties. Handle respectfully; medical conditions default to accommodation; follow venue alcohol/substance policies.

### 4.11. Rage-Quitting
**Def:** Intentionally abandoning ongoing game in anger/frustration without judge authorization (leaving table, refusing play, making game impossible to proceed).

**Examples:** Packing cards and leaving mid-game; refusing game actions ignoring judge; departing venue without notifying judge; scooping cards and walking away.

**Penalty:** **Disqualification**. Removed from game/event. Judge updates game state fairly. Time extension if significant disruption.

### 4.12. Failure to Play to Win
**Def:** Intentionally not pursuing own best possible result, distorting natural outcome.

**Playing to win (priority order):**
1. Attempt game win (strict VP majority, >=2 VP, more than any other)
2. If GW unrealistic, maximize VP
3. If no full VP possible, attempt half VP by surviving to time

Not required to succeed, required to genuinely pursue. Suboptimal play/miscalculation is not this infraction. Negotiation/deals permitted and unrestricted. A deal (even disadvantageous) is not infraction by itself. Infraction only if player knowingly executes deal that doesn't improve their chances at time made.

**Examples:**
- Refusing obvious oust for GW to favor/punish another player
- Making plays that knowingly reduce own chances without strategic justification
- Accelerating own elimination instead of surviving for half VP near timeout
- Openly "kingmaking," protesting, or acting from frustration
- Honoring deal that didn't improve chances when made
- Player with no minion, 3 pool, 30 min left: allowed to self-oust via 3 transfers
- Player with GW at 3VP, opponent at 0: allowed to concede rest for 3-2 victory
- Player with 2VP in duel with 1VP player: allowed to withdraw (2.5 to 2 GW); opponent allowed to let them if 1.5 timeout is inferior

Judges evaluate deals based on intent and reasonable perception at time made. Deal legal if accepting could reasonably improve chances. Exercise caution; disagreement with play line or hindsight not sufficient. Apply only when intent to abandon competitive goals is clear and demonstrable.

**Penalty:** **Warning**. **Standings Adjustment** in particularly clear/disruptive cases (explicit statements, deliberate throwing, repeated behavior).

## 5. Event Organization

### 5.1. Unexpected Drop
- During 5-player seating: convert to 4-player
- During 4-player seating: re-seat per V:EKN Tournament Rules 3.1.2
- Large events not using multi-judge system, drop at 4-player table after other rounds started: tournament official fills seat with random staff deck
- Drop causes inability to form legal tables: event immediately unsanctioned. Between rounds, may adjust (judge joins as player) to restore
- Drop before round 1, can't form legal tables: unsanctioned, no official prize support
- Unsanctioned due to mid-tournament drops: prizes may still be distributed. Submit report as usual noting what happened. Event appears unsanctioned, doesn't affect ratings.

### 5.2. Venue Interference
Adjust round times as needed. Event unsanctioned if: any round <2 hours AND at least one table ends due to time called. If <2hr round finishes with no tables reaching time limit, remains sanctioned. Note venue problems in report; prizes distributed normally if event completed.

### 5.3. Force Majeure / Emergency Termination
External events making continuation unsafe/impossible (fire, power failure, severe weather, threatening behavior, compromised safety).

Head Judge halts all games, ensures safety, then determines if continuation possible.

**A. Cannot continue:**
- No play if venue unsafe
- All games end, no further VP
- Players keep VP/GW from last fully completed round
- Partial rounds don't count (even if some tables finished)
- Final terminated -> incomplete, no winner unless policy requires
- Prizes/qualifications based on last completed round standings
- No completed rounds -> event void
- Head Judge submits full written report to VEKN

**B. Can safely resume:**
- Restart round from beginning (recommended), or
- Resume only if all tables unanimously agree and no competitive info compromised
- Any corrupted table or safety concern -> restart or terminate

**C. Hostile venue staff:**
- Treat as Force Majeure
- If unsafe, stop event, direct players to leave
- Relocation allowed only if: every player can relocate, no game info compromised, new space meets minimum standards
- If relocation impossible, terminate per Section A

### 5.4. Recommended Supplies
- **Sleeves:** Enough opaque sleeves for 2 full decks (library+crypt). Neutral color. For marked-card issues/emergency re-sleeving.
- **Replacement crypt cards:** Anarch Convert, group-appropriate low-impact vampires. Judge-issued proxies. Prioritize neutrality.
- **Spare library cards:** Blood Doll, Wake with Evening's Freshness, burn option/placeholder cards. Temporary, revertible.
- **Materials:** Pens, paper, printed tournament rules/conduct guide/ban+errata list, blank decklist forms.
- **Timing:** Stopwatch/timer for extensions/slow play/investigations. Tournament management system access.
- **Game aids:** Generic blood/pool counters, neutral tokens for game-state clarification.
